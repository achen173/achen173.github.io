MacLaurin Series for sin(x)

MacLaurin Series is an expansion of a function into infinite sums. With more sums, the better the approximation will be to the original function. The goal is increase the number of terms so much that it becomes indifferentiable comparatively. By looking at an example, sin(x), we will see how each additional sum/term relates to the composition of the orginal function.

Advanced Standard Encryption

Advanced Encyption Standard (AES) is a symmetric block cipher used to protect sensitive information. AES became the successor of Data Encryption Standard (DES) in 1997 after it began to show signs of vulnerabilities. Some benefits of AES include higher key sizes for more robustness against hacking and its publicly known encryption methodologies. The goal of this project, is to explore the entirety AES and to implement it on an Adafruit Board.
